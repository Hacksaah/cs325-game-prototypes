  • High Concept: You're trying to make it through the last day of school without failing and getting sent to summer school.
  • Theme: N/A
  • Mandated Variety: Users can jump with the UP cursor and the "Failings" are generated randomly. As the player progresses through the game, it gets more difficult to evade the F's. I guess this would be considered a side-view, escape/action game.
  • Prototype Goal: Smooth transition of difficulty. I didn't want to distract the player while increasing the difficulty of the game.
  • Player Experience Goals: I want players to feel the pressure and thrill of having to act in a consistent and precise manner throughout the whole game. The hitboxes on the "failings" are kind of wonky and hard to avoid if you're inconsistent.
  • Gameplay: A student is running through the hallways trying to escape and avoid failing on the last day of school. All the player has to do is avoid failing by jumping over the "failings" with the UP cursor. The player can either play as a male student or a female student and can switch characters after the play or play again as the same character.
  • Strategies: No stategy involved. Just good timing and consistency.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: A student is trying to get out of school on the last day of school. Unfortunately, failing might keep him/her in school for the summer. So as they try to escape school, teachers keep dropping work infront of them to do. The student wants to avoid this work at all cost. If the can't, they will get sent to summer school. It is your job to help make sure the student doesn't get sent to summer school.
  • Target Audience: Children in grade school who can relate to the feeling of trying to leave on the last day of school.
  • Play Time: Anywhere from 2 seconds to forever.
