Riddle Mystery

  • Originality: It is a multiplayer point and click adventure game in which players are fulfilling a story line together, but against each other.
  • Technical Merit: Although the code to make the point and click functionality work looks simple, it took 2 days of researching to figure it out. With that being said, point and click is hard to get right.
  • Prototype Postmortem: I learned that point and clicks look simple from an outside perspective, but coding it right is a painstakingly long process. The hardest part was trying to figure out how to get user text input. I found out that there is no easy way of doing it in Phaser, so I just skipped that part of the implementation.
  • Prototype Assets: Borrowed all of them.
    • Characters: Pacman, Metalface, and NPCs
    • Map: Desert
    • Music: Jeopardy Theme Song
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: It mostly resembles the game where I learned how to do the map setup only because the map is the exact same. The game mechanics, though, are completely different.
    • Camera follow: Camera Follow Example
    • Map setup: Maptile Example
    • Fading text: Tweens Example
  • High Concept: A multiplayer point and click adventure game in which you play against your friends.
  • Theme: Not relevant for this assignment
  • Mandated Variety: I chose a competitive multiplayer point and click genre.
  • Prototype Goal: The idea of competition in a "co-op style" story mode.
  • Player Experience Goals: I want them to feel as puzzled as I was making the game.
  • Gameplay: Player 1 is the person who points and clicks to move (also playing as pacman). Player 2 is playing as Metalhead, but doesn't have to control the movement since Metalhead just follows Pacman. The idea is to go talk to the 3 NPCs on the map and listen to their riddles. You only have 15 seconds to read the and process the riddle. Figure out the answers to all 3 riddles and find out what they mean. Scores are based on which players can answer the riddles. I couldn't figure out how to get user input in the form of text, so for now, just answer the riddles out loud with your partner.
  • Strategies: Be good at puzzles/riddles and problem solving.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: I want the riddles to be actual games that the players can play in against each other (requires scene changes which I didn't have time to figure out). Basically, when a player wins a challenge (answers a riddle in this case), they receive an item (score in this prototype) that helps them later on in the games story line. The player who wins more challenges, will have a more favorable outcome in the story line of the game. The story line itself has yet to be determined.
  • Target Audience: Competitive problem solvers.
  • Play Time: Shouldn't take longer than 3 minutes.
